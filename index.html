Intro

Hi, I'm Chenyao An. I'm currently pursuing an M.Eng. in Financial Technology at Duke University. I'm interested in applying data and machine learning to finance and risk problems—especially forecasting, risk management, and building practical tools that turn messy data into clear decisions.

Before Duke, I earned a B.S. in Data Science (Minor: Education) at the University of Wisconsin–Madison. My coursework spans probability & statistical inference, regression, machine learning, data visualization, SQL, and optimization.

What I'm focusing on

  • Machine learning in finance: forecasting, explainability, and model comparison
  • Risk & data modeling: turning high-frequency / multi-source data into indicators and insights
  • Product-oriented analytics: building tools that are simple, reliable, and user-friendly

Highlighted project

Realized Volatility Forecasting (DJIA, 2003–2024)
Built and compared models (Random Forest, XGBoost, LightGBM) for realized volatility forecasting using high-frequency data. Achieved around R² ≈ 0.63 and used SHAP to interpret key risk drivers.
